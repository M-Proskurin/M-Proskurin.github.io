In this preprint, we investigate how the rostral anterior cingulate cortex encodes latent structure of learned tasks. We find that ACC neural populations organize their representations as cognitive graphs — geometric structures that reflect the abstract relational knowledge the animal has acquired — providing a neural substrate for flexible, knowledge-guided behavior.
