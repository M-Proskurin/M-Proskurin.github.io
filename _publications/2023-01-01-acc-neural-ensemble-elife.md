This study examines how populations of neurons in the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) encode information about behavioral strategies. We find that the geometry of ACC neural ensemble dynamics reflects the prevalence of strategies in the animal's behavioral repertoire, suggesting that the ACC maintains an internal model of behavioral context that guides flexible decision-making.
