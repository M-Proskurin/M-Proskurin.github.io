I am a Scientific Computing Associate at [Janelia Research Campus](https://www.janelia.org/), HHMI, in Ashburn, VA. My background spans physics, neuroscience, and scientific software development — a path that has taken me from studying macromolecular biophysics to investigating the neural basis of flexible cognition.

My research has focused on how the brain encodes abstract knowledge and uses it to guide flexible, context-dependent behavior. I have worked extensively on the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) and its role in strategy selection and exploration. Using large-scale electrophysiology, optogenetics, and computational modeling, I study how neural population dynamics give rise to adaptive decision-making in behaving rodents.

I completed my Ph.D. in Neuroscience at [Johns Hopkins University](https://neuroscience.jhu.edu/) in 2020, working in the [Karpova Lab](https://www.janelia.org/lab/karpova-lab) at Janelia Research Campus. Before that, I earned a B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics and Physics from the [Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology](https://mipt.ru/en/) in 2011.

## Research Interests

- Neural basis of flexible decision-making and cognitive control
- Latent structure in large-scale neural population dynamics
- Anterior cingulate cortex and its role in exploration and strategy
- Scientific computing and data analysis tools for systems neuroscience
